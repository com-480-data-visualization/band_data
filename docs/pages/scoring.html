🤔 Why 15, 30, 40?

Tennis scoring comes from medieval France! The numbers may represent:

  • • Clock quarters: 15, 30, 45 (shortened to 40) minutes
  • • Movement on court: Players moved 15 feet closer to net with each point
  • • Betting stakes: Medieval French coins (15 sous increments)

Best of 3 Sets

  • • Most common format
  • • Used in regular ATP/WTA tours
  • • Women's Grand Slams
  • • Typical duration: 1.5-3 hours

Best of 5 Sets

  • • Men's Grand Slams only
  • • Davis Cup
  • • More physically demanding
  • • Can last 3-5+ hours

Special Situations

Deuce (40-40)

When both players reach 40 points, it's called "Deuce". Now someone must win by 2 points!

The Deuce Cycle:
Deuce → Player A wins point → Ad-In (server)
Deuce → Player B wins point → Ad-Out (receiver)

Tiebreak (6-6)

When a set reaches 6-6, play a tiebreak to 7 points (win by 2).

Tiebreak Rules:
  • • First to 7 points (must win by 2)
  • • Serve alternates every 2 points
  • • Switch sides every 6 points
  • • Winner takes the set 7-6
